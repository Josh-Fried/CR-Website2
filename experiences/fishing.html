FISHING

Explore The Waters of Guanacaste


World-Class Sportfishing

Nestled on the exclusive Punta Cacique peninsula, at the epicenter of Costa Rica's most celebrated sportfishing destination. This is not just a gateway to the Pacific; it's a launching point into the calm, protected waters of the Gulf of Papagayo, a region legendary for its incredible year-round fishery. Whether you are an elite angler seeking a new world record or a family looking for an unforgettable day on the water, your adventure begins here.

What makes this location truly special? The Gulf of Papagayo is a vast natural harbor, shielded by the Papagayo Peninsula to the north and the coastline to the east. Even during the "Papagayo winds" of the dry season (December-March), when other parts of the coast face rough seas, our waters remain famously calm. This means more comfortable days, fewer canceled trips, and a quicker, more pleasant ride to the fishing grounds. From here, you are just minutes from the main sportfishing hub of Playas del Coco, with direct access to both inshore and offshore action.


Inshore Adventures

You don't need to travel far for world-class trophies. The volcanic coastline, rocky islands, and estuaries right outside your bay are a prime habitat for the most iconic inshore species in the Pacific.

Roosterfish (Pez Gallo): This is the king of the inshore world. Famous for its distinctive "comb" and powerful fight, the Roosterfish is a bucket-list catch-and-release species that thrives in our waters.

Cubera Snapper: A true brawler of the reefs. Hooking one of these powerful, toothy snappers is a test of skill and strength.

Variety: The inshore waters are also teeming with Jack Crevalle, Mackerel, Red Snapper, and Grouper, ensuring a day of constant action.
